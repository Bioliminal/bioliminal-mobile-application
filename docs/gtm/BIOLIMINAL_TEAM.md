# The Bioliminal Team

Built by four of Gauntlet AI's most cracked members. Top of the class. Each
person owns a layer of the stack end-to-end.

## Why this team

No previous attempt at closing the EMG coaching loop had all four pieces on
the same team at the same time: hardware that can sense it, ML that can reason
over it, math that guarantees the reasoning holds, and a frontend that makes
the whole thing disappear behind a clean experience.

We met at the Gauntlet AI fellowship and couldn't stop working on this problem.

## The team

**Kelsi Andrews** — Mobile, CV Integration, Capture UX
Frontend developer building the premium interface that makes the underlying
stack feel invisible. Integrates the computer vision models into the app and
designs the capture UX around what the user actually needs — not what the
engineering team thinks is cool. If the experience isn't effortless, the
science underneath doesn't matter.

**Rajat Arora** — Hardware, Firmware, Signal Chain
Embedded systems engineer with hands-on experience designing chips from scratch.
Builds the physical layer that everything else depends on — the sEMG sensors,
the ESP32 signal chain, the haptic actuators, the BLE streaming pipeline. When
you're reading muscle activation in real time at consumer price points, the
hardware can't be approximate. Rajat's doesn't fail.

**Aaron Carney** — Biomechanics ML, Computer Vision, Server Pipeline
Published researcher (federated learning). The machine learning behind
Bioliminal isn't a black box bolted onto a sensor — it's backed by real
science, built by someone who publishes. Trains the CV models and builds the
server pipeline that turns a single phone video into joint moments, ground
reaction forces, and muscle forces. The science under the hood is actually
science.

**Rajiv Nelakanti** — Product, Cueing Thesis, Go-to-Market
Published game theorist. The math nerd. The cueing thesis — when to fire a cue,
which muscle, what modality, how to verify it worked — sits on top of critical
math. The sensors produce signal. The ML produces reasoning. Rajiv's math
ensures neither can fail silently. Holds the throughline from signal to
intervention to evidence.
