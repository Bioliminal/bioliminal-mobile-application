Tier 2 BioAmp EXG Pill × 4 — "Real Prototype"

~$40 for 4 boards ($10/channel)

What: Upside Down Labs BioAmp EXG Pill. Pill-size (25.4 × 10mm, <2g). Open-source (CERN-OHL-S). Publication-grade signal. This is what goes in the compression shirt.

Why this for the prototype: At $10/channel it makes 4 channels affordable. Tiny enough to embed in fabric. The noise floor (~5μV) is good enough for RMS fatigue tracking and L/R asymmetry detection.

Setup: Requires soldering pin headers + solder jumper bridge for EMG band config. Analog output → ESP32 ADC. 1–2 hours setup per board.

Signal quality: Comparable to clinical EMG with proper electrode placement and skin prep.

4 channels: Ch1 = Left erector spinae · Ch2 = Right erector spinae · Ch3 = Vastus lateralis · Ch4 = Vastus medialis

Tier 3 MyoWare 2.0 — "Clean Demo"

~$43.50

What: SparkFun MyoWare 2.0 (updated DEV-27924). Zero soldering. Snap-on electrodes directly onto the board. Three output modes: raw EMG, rectified, envelope.

Why this for demos: It just works. Out of the box. Adjustable gain knob. Built-in LED shield option shows activation visually. Extensive Arduino/ESP32 tutorials. This is the sensor you use when showing the concept to people who don't care about electronics — they care about the signal.

Setup: Stick on electrode pads, connect 3 wires (VIN, GND, SIG) to any microcontroller. Working signal in under 5 minutes.

Signal quality: Best out-of-box experience. 25–450Hz bandwidth. Envelope mode gives instant fatigue proxy without any signal processing code.
